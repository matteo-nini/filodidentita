# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Adults and young adults in Tuscany and across Italy interested in literature, arts, and cultural identity. They attend or consider attending the festival (Pontedera, San Miniato, Fucecchio, Montopoli) or follow it online. They are curious, culturally engaged, and expect a site that reflects the seriousness and warmth of the event itself, not a generic ticketing interface.

## Product Purpose

Filo d'identità is an annual literary and artistic festival exploring the theme of identity, held across four Tuscan towns each July. Now in its fifth edition (06–12 luglio 2026), the site is the festival's public face: it presents the program, archives past editions, communicates the team and mission, hosts the gallery, and opens a contact channel. Success means a visitor feels the character of the festival before reading a single event title, and leaves with a desire to be there.

## Brand Personality

Poetica, culturale, accessibile. A festival with a clear point of view, strong and inclusive identity, a genuine sense of community and human warmth. It takes positions. It is not neutral. The brand has matured: it is now also artigianale (handmade, organic, never sterile), radicata (rooted in place and season, Tuscan in its bones), and visivamente coraggiosa (willing to use color and form boldly, as a manifesto, not as decoration).

## Anti-references

- Generic festival / ticketing-platform aesthetics (Eventbrite-style card grids, institutional colors, boxed layouts)
- Corporate coldness (neutral palettes without character, detached tone of voice, design that prioritizes polish over personality)
- Overdesigned "brand system" cleanliness: the festival is handmade and lived-in, not a startup pitch deck

## Design Principles

1. **Identità prima di tutto.** Every design choice must reinforce the festival's distinct identity. Never default to what "cultural event sites" look like as a category.
2. **Forme vive, non rettangoli morti.** The brand's visual language uses organic, living forms: wavy edges, botanical silhouettes, thread motifs, grainy surfaces. Reject sterile rectangular containers where an organic alternative exists and serves the content.
3. **Territorio come radice.** The Tuscan setting is not decoration. Locality, seasonality, and a sense of place ground the aesthetic. The mineral teal of Italian pigments, the texture of handmade paper, the warm botanical light of July: these are the palette's reference objects, not abstract design trends.
4. **Prendere posizione.** The brand has opinions. Blank, hedge-everything design betrays the content. When in doubt, commit.
5. **La cultura è per tutti.** Openness to a broad public is a core value. WCAG AAA compliance is a design constraint that demands creative solutions, not a checkbox. Exclusionary aesthetics, insider-only visual language, and complexity for its own sake run against the festival's mission.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AAA compliance target. Special attention to: color contrast across the three-role palette (mineral teal, deep wine, muted magenta), motion preferences (respect `prefers-reduced-motion`), readable type sizing at all viewport widths. Multilingual audience consideration (Italian primary, some international visitors).

## Visual System Notes (for DESIGN.md)

Extracted from the 5th edition poster — use as inspiration, not literal application:

- **Three-role palette**: (1) mineral field — dusty teal/sage as a background or section field; (2) deep wine/plum for strong text and structural accents; (3) muted magenta/pink retained for logo, labels, and warmth.
- **Organic image treatment**: photos with wavy/scalloped bottom edges, not sharp rectangles. The cutout shape is part of the brand vocabulary.
- **Botanical silhouettes**: faint leaf/plant forms in backgrounds as texture, not decoration.
- **Thread/filo motif**: a drawn thread line element as a recurring graphic signature.
- **Typography addition**: a bold condensed serif for guest names and event titles (poster impact, not editorial). Bugaki remains for the brand logotype and display headings.
- **Surface texture**: subtle grain overlay on sections. Not applied uniformly; used where it reinforces the handmade quality.
